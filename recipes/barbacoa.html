Barbacoa Meat

Description

Similar to Mexican birria, barbacoa is more traditionally made from beef cheeks, but it can also be made from various cuts of beef, or even lamb, or goat. It really varies among the different regions of Mexico. But here’s the gist of it – it’s heavily seasoned meat with spices and chipotle peppers, then slow-cooked in a pit with coals or over an open flame until you’ve got beautifully juicy, fall-apart tender meat.

Ingredients

  • 3 pounds beef cheek meat
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 cup salt
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups water, or more as needed
  • 1/2 yellow onion, halved and thickly sliced
  • 3 gloves garlic, chopped

Steps

  1. Step 1 Coat beef cheek meat with olive oil. Rub salt, cumin, and pepper into meat. Wrap meat in aluminum foil and refrigerate, 4 hours to overnight.
  2. Step 2 Pour water into a slow cooker.
  3. Step 3 Arrange onion and garlic around beef cheek meat in the aluminum foil. Wrap foil tightly around meat and vegetables. Add a second sheet of aluminum foil around meat mixture, sealing tightly; place in the slow cooker.
  4. Step 4 Cook on Low, adding more water if it has evaporated, until meat is very tender, 7 to 8 hours. Remove foil packet from slow cooker and shred meat using 2 forks.
